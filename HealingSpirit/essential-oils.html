What Are Essential Oils?

Essential oils are the volatile liquids that are distilled from plants (including their respective parts such as seeds, bark, leaves, stems, roots, flowers, fruit, etc.). One of the factors that determines the purity and therapeutic value of an oil is its chemical constituents. These constituents can be affected by a vast number of variables: the part(s) of the plant from which the oil was produced, soil condition, fertilizer (organic or chemical), geographic region, climate, altitude, harvest season and method, and distillation process.

What benefits do pure, therapeutic-grade essential oils provide?

  1. Essential oils embody the regenerating, protective, and immune-strengthening properties of plants.
  2. Essential oil constituents are both small in molecular size and are also lipd soluble, allowing many of them to easily and quickly penetrate the skin.
  3. Essential oils are very powerful antioxidants. Antioxidants neutralize or quench free radicals
  4. Many essential oil constituents have the following properties:
    • antibacterial
    • antifungal
    • anti-infectious
    • antimicrobial
    • antitumor
    • antiparasitic
    • antiviral
    • antiseptic

How do I apply essential oils?

  1. Topically: Topical application is the process of placing an essential oil in the skin, hair, mouth, teeth, nails, or mucus membranes of the body.
  2. Aromatically: Aromatic application involves inhaling either a fine mist of the oil or a vapor of volatile aromatic compnents that have evaporated from the oil.
  3. Internally: Internally use is the process of consuming or otherwise internalizing an essential oil into the body.
