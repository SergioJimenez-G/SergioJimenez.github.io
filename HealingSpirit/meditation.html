Meditation

What is Meditation?

The term meditation is used to describe various meditative disciplines. The main purpose of these is to reduce stress levels and change the emotions of the person. It is believed that if a deep state of relaxation is achieved, can improve the health and well-being of a person.

Modern meditation practices are rooted in certain Asian religions and spiritual traditions. Today, thousands of people from different cultures around the world meditate, so if you are considering trying, you'll never be alone.

How to Practice?

When you meditate, usually sitting or lying quietly with eyes closed. Sometimes, you can repeat a sound or phrase (known as mantra) to help you focus. This discipline can do it anywhere and anytime you want.

There are two meditation practices:

  • "Full Conscience" Meditation.
  • Transcendental Meditation (MT)

"Full Conscience" Meditation

In this type of meditation it is encouraged changing the way we think, feel and act. The objective is to be free of negative thoughts and actions, and instead,strengthen and increase positive thoughts. To achieve a state of full consciousness, you learn to concentrate only on your breathing. If you manage to feel immersedhimself in this experience without reacting or judging what happens around him, he thinks that may be able to spread their thoughts and emotions in a more balancedway in their daily lives. Since this technique combines the practice of yoga meditation, will, in turn, benefits, and improved flexibility.

Transcendental Meditation (MT)

The TM technique is quite different from the previous one. During each session, all you have to do is repeat a mantra over and over again quietly or silently. This is done to not be distracted and to help you focus and find a state of total relaxation.

How Meditation Works?

Numerous studies suggest that meditation can have numerous health benefits. For example, if you have had a depression, a treatment called cognitive therapy based on mindfulness, it will help prevent another depression. In turn, studies indicate that full awareness meditation helps reduce stress, anxiety disorder or addictive behavior.

Scientists and scholars investigating the topic think that meditation works in such a way that affects the nervous system that controls the "answer fight-flight" (involuntary action in which the body responds to stress by increasing the heart and respiratory rate and the amount of sweat). However, this is not evidence today, so this theory can not say definitely.

Steps to Follow

  • Find a quiet place. Meditation requires focus, so you should avoid distractions; find a suitable place to relax and not be disturbed.
  • Feel comfortable. If you will be in a certain position for a long period of time, it is important to be as comfortable as possible.
  • Focus your attention. Breathe properly, or repeat a mantra will help you enter a state of meditation.
  • Keep an open mind. If this is the first time you try to practice meditation and unable to concentrate, remember that it takes practice.
